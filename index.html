Technical Review

Reasoning, limits, and supporting evidence

Evidence quality: Benchmarked
Problem
Spam detection needs robust classification under noisy, everyday text rather than idealized examples.
Contribution
Built the end-to-end pipeline, prepared text data, compared Word2Vec and GloVe embeddings, and evaluated five classifiers against a TF-IDF baseline.
Constraints
The comparison needed consistent preprocessing, interpretable baselines, and enough evaluation structure to avoid presenting one-off tuning as methodology.
Evaluation
Reached 93% accuracy, roughly 8 percentage points above TF-IDF. The repository includes precision, recall, F1, AUC, and a model comparison table.

Engineering Decisions

Choices grounded in documented project scope

Selected approach
Compared Word2Vec and GloVe representations across five classifiers through one consistent preprocessing and evaluation pipeline.
Alternatives in scope
TF-IDF served as an explicit baseline rather than an unmeasured alternative, making embedding-based improvement easier to assess.
Decision constraints
Noisy text and fair comparison favored repeatable preparation and the same evaluation structure across models.
What the work showed
A headline accuracy is more credible when the baseline, dataset scale, and multiple evaluation measures remain visible.

Technical Judgment

How I approach constrained, messy, real-world ML work.

I work from data quality, operating context, and evaluation discipline before model novelty. The goal is not just building something impressive in isolation, but building something credible under practical constraints.

01

Start from operating conditions

I scope technical work around what the system has to tolerate: noisy inputs, limited data consistency, hardware coupling, or real process constraints.

02

Separate claims from evidence quality

I distinguish between prototype, simulated, benchmarked, and applied results so the work stays honest about what was proven.

03

Show personal ownership clearly

For collaborative work, I isolate what I designed or implemented so reviewers can judge my contribution without guessing.

04

Prefer evaluation over hype

I treat baselines, measurable outcomes, and practical usefulness as more important than fashionable models without a clear reason.
